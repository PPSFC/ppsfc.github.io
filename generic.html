What is the relevance of his ideas to curriculum in Jamaica?


  • Teachers should not only impart knowledge, but also to change the linear way of students’ thinking formed from years of examination-oriented education. Hence, curriculum is more centered around increasing rigor within the classrooms. Teacher are encouraged to ask more open-ended questions that promote higher order thinking rather than reciting important points.


  • Influenced a change from the UK based GCE examinations to a Caribbean based CXC examination that is more in line with the cultural experiences of the Jamaican people. This is in keeping with Doll's 4R's, specifically relation and recursion that speaks to the need to relate cultural experience to the curriculum.


  • Doll’s curriculum theory influenced consistent evolution of the curriculum and course materials, for example the changes in curriculum types: Rose Curriculum, Revised Primary Curriculum, National Standard Curriculum (NSC). The National Standard Curriculum acknowledges that knowledge is not a fixed quality, rather it changes and individuals are able to generate new knowledge.


  • Doll’s Curriculum theory is relevant to curriculum ideas in jamaica. This can be seen in its Influence on changes in assessment methods and modalities. The introduction of School Based Assessment, Internal Assessments and greater emphasis on Research at the post secondary level. This enables greater flexibility for students to complete assessments in comparison to traditional public examinations which mostly tests students ability to recall information.


  • Influence changes in teaching strategies, that is, a thrust away from the teacher centered approach to teaching and learning.
